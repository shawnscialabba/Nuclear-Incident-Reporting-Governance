# Nuclear-Incident-Reporting-Governance

### Summary

Directed the formal incident reporting program, ensuring strict adherence to the licensing basis and regulatory requirements (NAVSEA/NRRO standards, aligned with 10 CFR 50 App B/NQA-1 expectations). Managed event responses for high-risk scenarios (contamination events, RAM-loss investigations, chemistry excursions).

### Objectives & Scope

*   Ensure strict compliance with licensing basis and regulatory reporting requirements.
*   Author and verify formal incident reports that include conditions, safety status, probable cause, contributing cause, root cause analysis (RCA), and corrective actions.
*   Incorporate findings into Command Audit & Surveillance (A&S) plans and quarterly engineering assessments for strategic awareness.

### Achievements & Impact

*   **Deficiency Reduction:** The rigorous approach to incident investigation and documentation ensured audit readiness and drove a **40% reduction** in recurring performance deficiencies through targeted corrective actions.
*   **Compliance:** Ensured strict adherence to reporting standards, safeguarding integrity and accountability.
*   **Continuous Improvement:** Utilized RCA and A&S processes to drive systemic improvements and prevent recurrence of high-risk scenarios.
